About Me

I was born in Munich and grew up in Hamburg, where I completed my high school diploma. At the age of 15, I moved to Orotina, Costa Rica for a high school exchange. I also spent a social year in Trujillo, Peru before studying in Passau and eventually moving to Magdeburg. I am currently a visiting researcher in Melbourne.

Early Work Experience

Since the age of 16, I have worked a wide variety of jobs, an experience that, in hindsight, may well have fuelled my curiosity for behavioral economics. These include working at Junge Bäckerei; as a Spanish tutor; as a secretary at Team Rasmussen translation agency; as a waitress at Shamrock Irish Pub and at several Bierfeste; as an after-school care supervisor at Grundschule Kamminer Straße in Hamburg; as an intern in capital market analysis at Hamburger Sparkasse; as a research assistant at the Chair in Economics of Professor Johann Graf Lambsdorff; and as a German as a Foreign Language (DaF) teacher to people from various cultural backgrounds, including Honduras, Finland, and China.
