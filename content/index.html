I am a Research Associate and PhD candidate in Economics at the Faculty of Management and Economics, University of Magdeburg, supervised by Karim Sadrieh. I am currently a visiting researcher at the University of Melbourne, hosted by Nisvan Erkal and Max Grossmann.

Research

I do research in behavioral economics, experimental economics, and political economy, using laboratory experiments to study institutional trust, cooperation, and belief formation.

Giving a Voice

Increasing Individual Self-Expression to Enhance Group Welfare and the Resilience to System Disbelief

Can giving people a voice prevent them from losing faith in the system? This paper investigates whether increased opportunities for self-expression can strengthen group welfare and build resilience against system disbelief.

Feeling Forgotten – The Rise of System Disbelief

with Karim Sadrieh

What happens when people feel overlooked by the systems meant to serve them? This research explores the origins and dynamics of system disbelief, tracing how feeling forgotten can erode trust in institutions.
